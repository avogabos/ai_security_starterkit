Dear Steve,
As you probably know, there's a requirement for every company registered in the 
Republic of Ireland to have at least one Director who is resident in the EEA.
Indeed, not having such a Director is one of the 127 "indictable" offences that 
Auditors are obliged to report to the Director of Corporate Enforcement once they
become aware of it.
The alternatives to having a Director who resides in the EEA are:

* To either take up a Section 137 Bond (click here [http://r20.rs6.net/tn.jsp?f=001FT6ABSxuai3nrs1oEnq_7ZxLdQwxOEHkGHwJovj234b1L1JbODQOnmHDmeKKjOQ9-MPTY4MHsCLSQsYRZOqWMqAiXO1GGAgh8pvtdpY7mtNZbQsX478bMRjMiS4OfmbyPrVKlkiiR2jaFvgu07koZx5yOkpZwhwWEhinvgXHqcg7obW-park6afZVihbMwlNuw02AhlYEMJHgW2H9crFA0LG6Ih0X9Rk&c=ScOs-NbNRGkjUheU4g3Sv4rOvJR2jrPRNgOvlV97VV9ZkhZsxgKh9w==&ch=ALABecFyJW7xBgSuqYlsZFTKU5MQAKHoIml8TveLMlEftMmHBJ7Xdw==]
for details)
or

* To apply to the Revenue Commissioners for an exemption, based on the company having
a "real and continuous economic link with the State"(click here [http://r20.rs6.net/tn.jsp?f=001FT6ABSxuai3nrs1oEnq_7ZxLdQwxOEHkGHwJovj234b1L1JbODQOnmHDmeKKjOQ97glS6OzCK8L4yhqHNZvUNn9mf1h_S47WxBvjayOoiVwZfXLoJNE6k1JGu8Mgwhg3m3LLWsA5s0gsOebssS0El4VAUzYwywIQRn1aapqNBpMuhCiOU-XXdCxZ4PCYCyUTh1u40npuKhQH-scDB9VpqTapvzXU2SkJ&c=ScOs-NbNRGkjUheU4g3Sv4rOvJR2jrPRNgOvlV97VV9ZkhZsxgKh9w==&ch=ALABecFyJW7xBgSuqYlsZFTKU5MQAKHoIml8TveLMlEftMmHBJ7Xdw==]
for details)
CFI can assist in arranging either a Section 137 Bond or in making application to
the Revenue Commissioners for an exemption.
In limited circumstances, we can also arrange for the appointment of a Director 
who is resident in the Rep. of Ireland (subject to terms and conditions) to act.
